#### DevLog 2, 2026-06-08:

I created a new repository titled "TelluriumProjects" and created a virtual environment within it. I installed Tellurium onto this venv and copied the quickstart prompt in the notebook firstmodel.ipynb [(View on GitHub)](https://github.com/willtsharp/TelluriumProjects/blob/main/ModelNotebooks/firstmodel.ipynb), which is discussed both at the end of the first lecture and at <a href="https://tellurium.readthedocs.io/en/latest/quickstart.html#simple-example">Quick Start</a>. 

I was surprised by the clarity and intuitiveness of the Antimony syntax. Writing reactions and reaction rates was not difficult, but produced interesting graphs depicting how the species changed. One aspect I am interested in learning more about is the units within these oridnary differential equation systems and the graphs depicting them. Without units to contextualize things, the simulations don't mean much. I am looking forward to identifying and modeling a real network using experimental rates that give results that can be interpreted in a real life context.
