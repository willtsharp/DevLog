#### DevLog 1, 2026-06-04:

After jumpstarting the Biological Network Modeling (BNM) project, I found myself wanting to record a log of my learning and progress. This series of blog posts serves to do exactly that. 

Hence far, I have mainly sought to determine what I wanted this project to look like. I grew curious about computationally modeling phenomena such as drug toxicology, immune responses, and wound healing, and sought out resources to explore. I setteled on a very brief introduction to ordinary and partial differential equations on youtube before jumping into ENG340/542. The lecture-style videos combined with the introduction to Tellurium (given my previous experince with python and Jupyter Notebooks) felt like a good fit.

I watched the first lecture when determining that this series would be a good fit, and plan to return to follow along with the Tellurium introduction, and practice thinking in terms of (biological) networks.
